# Categories of SubFederal Laws

_Any law can be positive or negative._
_Current as of March 13, 2025._

This is the authoritative ICI type/subtype taxonomy. Subtype codes are globally
unique — each number belongs to exactly one type. Base points are per type
(P=4; D/E/H/T=3; B=2; L/W/V=1), with the subtype-level overrides noted below
(B/12 and B/14 = 1 point; E/18,19,20,21,62 = 2 points).

The machine-readable encoding used by the pipeline for validation lives in
`pipeline/taxonomy.py`.

## Detailed definitions

### P — Law Enforcement (4 points)

**1. 287(g) agreement** — Laws, resolutions, or agreements that require or prohibit local/state agencies from entering into 287(g) agreements with ICE; also the actual entering into a 287(g) MOA. Positive laws terminate or reject 287(g) agreements; negative laws require or renew them.

**2. Secure Communities** — Local participation in the Secure Communities program (fingerprint data sharing from local jails with ICE). Positive laws restrict/terminate cooperation; negative laws reflect full or increased cooperation.

**3. Immigration Crimes** — Laws that define, create, or eliminate local/state-level immigration-related criminal offenses (e.g., making it a crime to be undocumented, or prohibiting treating civil immigration violations as criminal).

**4. Conditions/punishments for immigration crimes or immigrants** — Laws altering treatment of immigrants in the criminal justice system based on immigration status (e.g., 364-day sentencing to avoid automatic deportation, or status-based sentencing enhancements).

**5. Checking immigration status, arrest based on immigration status** — Whether/when local officers may inquire about, verify, or act on immigration status. Positive laws prohibit status inquiries/arrests absent another violation; negative laws authorize/require status checks.

**6. Other compensation** — Grants/funding that enable or incentivize local enforcement participation (Operation Stonegarden, JAG grants with immigration conditions, etc.).

**7. Funding** — Laws directing, restricting, or allocating funding specifically for immigration enforcement by local employees.

**8. Other** — Catch-all for law-enforcement immigration laws not fitting a specific P subcategory.

**36. School district/university policies** — Immigration-related policies of schools/universities (collecting/sharing student status, cooperating with ICE on premises).

**37. Detainers used to enforce immigration laws** — Whether jails/agencies honor ICE detainer requests. Positive laws limit/refuse detainers (esp. without a court order); negative laws require/permit compliance.

**38. Release immigration information on individual detainees (including release date)** — Whether agencies share detainee custody status/release dates with ICE. Positive laws prohibit/restrict; negative laws require/allow.

**39. Use of government resources to enforce immigration laws/cooperation** — Broad restriction/authorization of using local resources (personnel, equipment, databases, facilities) to assist federal enforcement.

**40. Not allow ICE/CBP to interview in local jail** — Whether ICE/CBP may access local jails to interview people in custody. Positive laws bar/restrict; negative laws permit/facilitate.

**41. Transit authorities** — Immigration policies of local/regional transit authorities (whether staff enforce immigration law or cooperate with ICE).

**42. Joint operations** — Participation in joint operations/task forces with ICE. Positive laws prohibit/limit; negative laws authorize/require.

**43. Private employer ability to cooperate** — Laws addressing private employers' ability to cooperate with immigration enforcement.

**44. Jail space** — Contracts/arrangements for local jail/detention space to house federal immigration detainees. Positive laws terminate/restrict; negative laws establish/maintain.

**45. Courts and other sensitive locations** — Presence/activity of immigration agents at sensitive locations, esp. courthouses (identification requirements, arrest restrictions, staff monitoring).

**46. Hotels** — Immigration policies for lodging establishments (e.g., barring disclosure of guests' immigration info without legal process).

**47. T/U/SIJS Visas** — Local certification cooperation for T visas (trafficking), U visas (crime victims), and Special Immigrant Juvenile Status.

**48. Basic Ordering Agreements** — Basic Ordering Agreements (BOAs) between local sheriffs and ICE to prevent release of undocumented immigrants with criminal records; distinct from full 287(g) or jail-space contracts.

**49. Immigration Counsel** — Laws/EOs providing or facilitating access to immigration legal counsel/advisors (e.g., immigration adviser positions in prosecutors' offices).

**50. Warning** — Officials/agencies proactively warning immigrant communities of upcoming ICE operations/sweeps/checkpoints.

**51. License Plates & Facial Recognition Technology & Databases** — Whether agencies share surveillance data (license plate readers, facial recognition, databases) with ICE. Positive laws restrict/prohibit; negative laws permit/require.

**53. Subpoenas** — Use of subpoenas in immigration enforcement (whether local agencies must respond to immigration-related subpoenas, rules on compelling info).

**59. Prosecutorial discretion / immigration-neutral convictions / consider immigration consequences / limits on inquiring about immigration status** — Prosecutor/court policies to consider immigration consequences in plea bargaining/sentencing, seek immigration-neutral convictions, and refrain from status inquiries in proceedings.

**60. Sanctuary policies** — Broad sanctuary policies declaring the government won't use local resources to assist federal enforcement absent a public-safety threat or legal requirement. Distinguished from narrower subcategories.

**61. Probation officer cooperation with ICE** — Policies governing probation-officer cooperation with ICE (narrower than general cooperation).

**65. DNA** — Collection/retention/sharing of DNA intersecting with immigration enforcement (e.g., DNA samples from those with immigration detainers).

**67. State Orders of Removal** — State-level mechanisms to issue or facilitate orders directing immigrants to leave, or voluntary-departure frameworks.

**68. Private enforcement / private right of action / enforcement mechanism** — Mechanisms for private individuals to file complaints or sue to enforce immigration-related ordinances.

**69. Government funding depends on compliance** — Conditioning receipt of funding on compliance with (or limits on) immigration enforcement (e.g., withholding funds from sanctuary jurisdictions).

**70. Criminal penalties against government for violations (includes immunity)** — Penalties on officials who violate immigration enforcement mandates, or immunity/indemnification for officials who comply with state enforcement law.

**71. State and local authorities required to report to state** — Requiring agencies to report immigration-enforcement activity/data to a state authority.

**72. Law enforcement transportation of immigrants to federal detention facilities** — Whether local agencies may/must transport immigrants to federal detention. Negative laws require/authorize; positive laws prohibit.

**73. Required to report undocumented persons to the federal government** — Requiring officials to report known/suspected undocumented individuals or cooperate in identifying/removing them. Positive laws prohibit; negative laws mandate.

**74. State parole laws/programs to deport immigrants more quickly** — Using parole/supervised release to facilitate/accelerate deportation (e.g., detainers as a parole condition).

**76. Participate in immigration sweeps** — Whether local enforcement may/must participate in federal immigration sweeps/operations. Positive laws prohibit; negative laws authorize/require.

**77. Judicial review of detainer requests** — Requiring judicial review/approval of ICE detainer requests before a jail may comply.

### B — Benefits (2 points)

**9. General Eligibility** — Broad laws on immigrant eligibility for city/county services and benefits (not program-specific).

**10. Education** — Immigrant access to educational programs/services/resources (libraries, ESL, civic education, city-funded services).

**11. Welfare (Medical, Cash assistance, Housing)** — Immigrant access to Medicaid/healthcare assistance, cash assistance, public housing, general welfare.

**12. Non-work-related licenses (e.g., Guns) — 1 point** — Access to non-employment licenses/permits/credentials (concealed-carry permits, marriage licenses). Weighted 1 point.

**34. ID cards** — Municipal ID card programs available regardless of immigration status.

**14. Other — 1 point** — Catch-all for benefits laws (offices/commissions/programs supporting immigrant integration). Weighted 1 point.

**15. Funding** — Allocating/restricting/conditioning funding for immigrant welfare/benefits (refugee resettlement, humanitarian aid).

**52. Refugee (resettlement)** — Laws/resolutions on refugee resettlement (welcoming or declining).

**54. Release or collect immigration information** — Whether agencies/employees may collect/share/release immigration-status info while providing services (as opposed to law-enforcement contexts under P).

**58. Immigration detention facilities/conditions** — Conditions under which immigrants are held (humane treatment, counsel, healthcare, oversight of facilities housing minors).

**64. Keeping immigration information secure (see B54)** — Secure storage/handling of lawfully collected immigration-status info (e.g., protecting alternative driver's-license data from enforcement use).

### D — Driver's licenses (3 points)

**13. Drivers Licenses** — All laws on immigrant access to driver's licenses. Negative laws prohibit undocumented immigrants from obtaining licenses; positive laws allow licenses regardless of status.

### E — Employment (3 points)

**16. General Eligibility** — General eligibility to work (E-Verify mandates, prohibitions on hiring undocumented workers, business-license conditions).

**17. Government Contracts** — Conditioning government contracts on immigration employment requirements (E-Verify, certifications).

**22. Funding** — Allocating/restricting/conditioning funding for employment-related immigrant programs/enforcement.

**56. Employment Discrimination** — Prohibiting employment discrimination based on immigration status.

**57. ICE Interactions** — Employer/labor-agency interactions with ICE (protections against threats to report status in retaliation).

**75. Private right of action / complaint mechanism** — Complaint/enforcement mechanisms for immigration-related employment violations.

### E — Employment (2 points)

**18. Conditions (compensation, tax)** — Wages, minimum wage, tax, wage-theft protections as applied to immigrant workers.

**19. Professional Licenses** — Whether immigrants (esp. undocumented/DACA) may obtain professional licenses.

**62. DACA Professional Licenses** — Whether DACA recipients specifically may obtain professional licenses.

**20. Day Labor Camps** — Day-labor centers, solicitation regulations, hirer–worker rules.

**21. Other** — Catch-all for employment-related immigration laws.

### L — Language (1 point)

**23. English only** — Declaring English the official language; English-only government business/forms/signage.

**24. Other/cultural** — Multilingual access, cultural inclusion, language services (non-English-only).

**25. Funding** — Funding for language-access programs, translation, ESL.

### H — Housing (3 points)

**26. Landlord Ordinances** — Requiring/prohibiting landlords from verifying tenants' immigration status.

**27. Maximum Occupancy** — Occupancy limits (often targeting shared immigrant housing).

**28. Other** — Catch-all for housing-related immigration laws.

**29. Funding** — Funding for housing programs as they relate to immigrant access.

### W — Law related (1 point)

**30. Restrictions on notary publics and other non-lawyers** — Regulating non-attorney immigration service providers ("notario fraud").

**31. Legal services, including legal defense funds** — Establishing/funding immigrant legal services and removal-defense funds.

**35. Other** — Catch-all for law-related immigration provisions.

### V — Vote related (1 point)

**32. Conditions/qualifications for voting (proof of citizenship, etc.)** — Altering conditions under which noncitizens may vote (expanding local voting rights, or proof-of-citizenship requirements).

**33. Campaign contributions** — Whether immigrants may make campaign contributions.

### T — Transportation (3 points)

**63. Government Contracts** — Whether transportation contracts may go to companies that cooperate with immigration enforcement / transport immigrants to detention.

**66. Transporting immigrants** — Transportation of migrants by charter buses/private carriers (advance-notice requirements, penalties for uncoordinated drop-offs).
